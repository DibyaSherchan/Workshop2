Thomas J. Watson

About him

Thomas Jhon. Watson was an American businessman who became a CEO of IBM. He was able make his company grow into an international force from the span of 1914 to 1956. He developed the IBM's management style and corporate culture, which he learnt by training at NCR(National Cash Register) under Jhon Henry Patterson. He was able to achieve a higly selling organization with the help of just a punched card tabulating machines. Throughout his life he was called the world's greatest salesman but he passed away in 1956.


NCR

The way he got to know about NCR is when he aquired a fresh NCR cash register in his butcher shop. He had to arrange and transfer the installment payments to the new owner of the butcher shop. Since he got more intruigued about NCR he went to the company for a visit which is when he met Jhon J. Range and asked him for a job. He pecked Range's calls repeatedly, with a number of abortive attempts. Until he finally was finally hired in November 1896, as a sales apprentice to Range.
